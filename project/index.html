Hedy Lamarr: A Genius Ahead Of Her Time

Home Process Team 17

Who?

Hedy Lamarr was an Austrian-American actress renowned for her beauty in the mid-19002. Below that beauty was a remarkable brain that often went unnoticed in her career.

What?

Lamarr invented a frequency-hopping signal intended to mask torpedos during World War II to combat Axis forces. Though it wasn't implemented at the time, this technology became the basis for what would become WiFi, GPS, and other staples of life as we know it today.

Why?

As an actress, Lamarr was often objectified, yet her mind lead to lilfe as we know it today. She deserves more recognition than a quiet, posthumous addition to the National Inventor's Hall of Fame. Her achievements proves that women aren't either 'a pretty face' or 'the brain' in life; real people aren't so one-dimensional.
